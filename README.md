# -SALES-PERFORMANCE-ANALYSIS

**Project Overview**
------
This data analysis project evaluates sales performance across four Nigerian cities, identifies top performing products, sales reps and regions, and generates insights that support data-driven business decisions. This project was completed as part of my learning journey as I transition into a data analyst role.

**Data Sources**

The primary dataset used for this analysis is the “Sales Performance.xlsx” file, containing detailed sales transaction records across Lagos, Kano, Port Harcourt, and Abuja.

**Dataset Description**

The dataset contains 2098 sales transaction records with the following fields:

	∙	Date, Month and Day
  
	∙	Region and City
  
	∙	Customer Type (Retail / Corporate)
  
	∙	Sales Channel (Store / Online)
  
	∙	Product and Category
  
	∙	Unit Price and Cost Price
  
	∙	Quantity
  
	∙	Revenue, COGS and Profit
  
	∙	Sales Rep
  
**Data Cleaning/Preparation**

In the initial data preparation phase, I did the following tasks:

	∙	Checked for missing and inconsistent values across all columns
  
	∙	Ensured numerical columns such as Revenue, COGS and Profit were correctly formatted
  
	∙	Added calculated columns for Revenue, COGS and Profit using Excel formulas
  
	∙	Standardized column names for clarity and consistency
  
**Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**

The exploratory data analysis was conducted to understand the structure of the data, assess data quality, and uncover key sales patterns. It focused on answering the following business questions:

	∙	What is the overall revenue, profit and cost performance?
  
	∙	Which products generate the highest profit
  
	∙	Which regions and cities contribute the most to revenue?
  
	∙	Which sales rep is driving the most revenue?
  
	∙	How are sales distributed across channels and customer types?
  
	∙	Which month recorded the lowest customer activity and why?
  
**Results/Findings**

	∙	Total revenue came in at ₦2.33B across 2098 transactions with a 20% profit margin
  
	∙	Laptop A13 was the most profitable product, generating ₦105M in profit far ahead of every other product
  
	∙	Electronics dominated category revenue at ₦1.33B, more than Furniture and Home Appliances combined
  
	∙	The East region led all four regions with ₦643M in revenue
  
	∙	Peter topped the sales rep leaderboard at ₦434M,the gap between the top and bottom rep was only ₦66M  showing a well-balanced team
  
	∙	Store sales (₦1.24B) outperformed Online sales (₦1.09B), challenging the assumption that digital always wins
  
	∙	May recorded only 18 transactions compared to a monthly average of 520 a critical anomaly that requires investigation
  
**Recommendations**

Based on the analysis, I recommend the following:

	∙	Double down on Electronics, specifically Laptops and Desktops, as they are the highest profit drivers  inventory and marketing decisions should reflect this

	∙	Investigate the May anomaly urgently  18 transactions against a 520 transaction monthly average is either a data issue or a real business problem that cannot be ignored
  
	∙	Rethink channel investment strategy  store sales are outperforming online, so physical channel investment should not be reduced without understanding the reason behind this gap
  
	∙	Review low-margin products like Blender B10 (₦6.9M profit) and Microwave M20 (₦13.4M profit) consider a pricing review, bundling strategy, or discontinuation

	∙	Investigate the South region gap  at ₦530M, it trails the East (₦643M) by over ₦113M, and understanding that gap could unlock meaningful growth
  
**Limitations**

	∙	The dataset covers only January to May 2025 and may not capture full-year or long-term sales trends
  
	∙	May data appears incomplete, which affected monthly trend analysis
  
	∙	External factors such as promotions, seasonal demand, or economic conditions were not included in the dataset
  
**Outcome**

This project provided clear insights into sales performance across Nigerian cities and strengthened my skills in data cleaning, KPI calculation, pivot table analysis, dashboard design, and business insight communication using Microsoft Excel.
